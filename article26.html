Ulcerative Colitis Symptoms May Be Treated with Probiotics
Extracted from Enteric microbiota leads to new therapeutic strategies for ulcerative colitis, available at PubMed.gov

Ulcerative colitis is a disorder is marked by chronic inflammation and the formation of ulcers in the innermost layer of the intestines. It can prove painful and frustrating to live with, especially when ordinary treatments fail to provide relief.

While there is no known cure for the disorder, studies show that probiotics and other new therapies aid in decreasing inflammation in the gut. How are these therapies different from conventional approaches that do not seem to work for most people? These therapies target the gut microbiome, which is naturally diverse and incredibly beneficial in maintaining natural body processes like digestion and immunity.

Ulcerative colitis disrupts the balance of microorganisms living in the gut which leads to discomfort, chronic inflammation and poor immune function. By restoring a natural balance to the gut microbiota, the body can better regulate these primal systems and reduce the pain and inflammation associated with ulcerative colitis.

Probiotics Reduce the Prevalence of the Common Cold/Flu During Times of Stress
Extracted from Bifidobacterium bifidum R0071 results in a greater proportion of healthy days and a lower percentage of academically stressed students reporting a day of cold/flu: a randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled study, available at PubMed.gov

Acute psychological stress is positively associated with the common cold/flu. When the body is stressed, the immune system can become overwhelmed.

Fortunately, recent studies have shown that probiotics can help reduce the impact of the common cold/flu on people experiencing acute episodes of high stress. The Food Science and Human Nutrition Department of the University of Florida conducted a study to determine the efficacy of probiotics in preventing days of cold/flu symptoms in academically stressed students. The percentage of students who reported cold/flu symptoms during the 6 week trial period was significantly lower in those who were given particular probiotic strains.

Probiotics have been associated with improved immune system functions, which may be the mechanism behind preventing the common cold/flu during times of acute stress. If you are someone who experiences frequent stress episodes, probiotics may be key in helping you maintain your healthy immune system!
